# Introduction
From the many insights I absorbed from many books, here I want to share an overview to give you a recommendation on what this book is about to convey to its readers.

### Let's follow us from first to bottom
## Mindset - Updated Edition: Changing The Way You Think To Fulfil Your Potential
![img](https://image.gramedia.net/rs:fit:0:0/plain/https://cdn.gramedia.com/uploads/items/img183.jpg)
**Topic of Gendre:** Psychology/Self-Help/Personal Development/Education/Success.  
**Overview:**  
Carol S. Dweck's "Mindset" is a groundbreaking book that introduces the powerful concepts of fixed and growth mindsets, showing how these fundamental beliefs about our abilities dramatically impact our success and happiness. A fixed mindset believes talents are innate and unchangeable, leading to avoidance of challenges, while a growth mindset sees abilities as developable through effort and learning, fostering resilience and a love of improvement. Dweck provides compelling research and practical strategies for shifting from a limiting fixed mindset to an empowering growth mindset, enabling readers to unlock their full potential in all areas of life, from education and career to relationships and personal growth.

## Good habits Bad habits
![img](https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/415WoPigEgL._SY445_SX342_.jpg)
**Topic of Gendre:** Self-Help/Psychology/Personal Development/Behavioral Science/Mindset.  
**Overview:**  
This book dives into the fascinating world of habits, exploring the fundamental difference between those that propel us forward and those that hold us back. It goes beyond simply labeling habits as "good" or "bad," instead dissecting the underlying psychology of how they form, why we stick to them, and their profound impact on our daily lives, productivity, and overall well-being. The author likely unpacks practical strategies for identifying destructive patterns, dismantling them, and consciously building positive routines that align with our goals and values. It’s an insightful guide for anyone looking to understand the mechanics of their own behavior and actively shape their future by mastering the art of habit formation.

## How to Win Friends & Influence People (Dale Carnegie Books)
![img](https://images-na.ssl-images-amazon.com/images/S/compressed.photo.goodreads.com/books/1442726934i/4865.jpg)
**Topic of Gendre:** Social Skills/Self-Help/Communication/Psychology.  
**Overview:**  
This book is a total classic for anyone who wants to get better at dealing with people. It's packed with super practical advice on how to make friends, get people to like you, and even influence others without being pushy. Carnegie basically breaks down human nature and gives you simple, actionable tips on things like really listening, making people feel important, and seeing things from their perspective. It's not about being fake; it's more about understanding how people tick so you can build better relationships, whether it's at work or in your personal life. If you've ever wanted to improve your social game or just be more effective in your interactions, this book is a fantastic starting point.

## The Charisma Myth: How Anyone Can Master the Art and Science of Personal Magnetism
![img](https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/81hS0u0bqXL._SY522_.jpg)
**Topic of Gendre:** Social Skills/Self-Help/Psychology/Communication/Leadership.  
**Overview:**  
"The Charisma Myth" debunks the idea that charisma is something you're born with, instead presenting it as a learnable skill that anyone can develop. Olivia Fox Cabane breaks down charisma into understandable components like presence, power, and warmth, offering practical, actionable techniques and exercises to enhance each one. This book provides a clear roadmap to becoming more influential, engaging, and inspiring in any situation, whether you're aiming to boost your social confidence or lead more effectively.

## The power of your subconscious mind

**Topic of Gendre:** Self-Help/Spirituality/Psychology/Mindset/Personal Growth.

## In Sheep's Clothing: Understanding and Dealing with Manipulative People
![img](https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/41L8k1wLDBL._SY445_SX342_.jpg)
**Topic of Gendre:** Psychology/Relationships/Self-Help/Communication/Boundaries.  
**Overview:**  
really insightful book that helps you spot and deal with manipulative people, especially those who often pretend to be innocent or helpful while secretly controlling others. George K. Simon, a clinical psychologist, explains the common tactics manipulators use – things like guilt trips, playing the victim, or subtle deception – and how they exploit your vulnerabilities. It's not just about identifying these behaviors, but also about giving you practical tools and strategies to protect yourself, set healthy boundaries, and stop being a target without getting caught in their emotional games. If you've ever felt used or controlled in a relationship and couldn't quite put your finger on why, this book offers clear explanations and empowering solutions.

## Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion
![img](https://images-na.ssl-images-amazon.com/images/S/compressed.photo.goodreads.com/books/1391026083i/28815.jpg)
**Topic of Gendre:** Psychology/Persuasion/Marketing/Sales/Social Science.   
**Overview:**  
"Influence" is a landmark book that breaks down the six universal principles of persuasion that drive people to say "yes." Robert Cialdini, a renowned psychologist, spent years studying how compliance works, from salespeople to cult recruiters, and distills his findings into powerful concepts like reciprocity, commitment and consistency, social proof, authority, liking, and scarcity. He provides countless real-world examples to illustrate how these principles are expertly (and sometimes unethically) used to influence behavior, equipping readers not only to be more persuasive themselves but also to defend against manipulative tactics in everyday life and business.


## The Psychology of Money
![img](https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/81wZXiu4OiL._SY522_.jpg)
**Topic of Gendre:** Finance/Psychology/Behavioral Economics/Life Lessons/Decision Making.  
**Overview:**  
"The Psychology of Money" isn't your typical finance book; instead of formulas and spreadsheets, Morgan Housel dives into why people behave the way they do with money, arguing that financial success is often less about what you know and more about your behavior. He explores fascinating stories and examples that highlight our biases, emotions, and personal histories, showing how these factors profoundly influence everything from saving and investing to risk-taking. It's a super insightful read that helps you understand common money mistakes and build better financial habits by realizing that good investing isn't necessarily about being smart, but about being consistently rational and patient.

## An Inquiry into the Nature and Causes of the Wealth of Nations
### Or called: The wealth of nation
![img](https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/512xJ3lNsfL._SY445_SX342_.jpg)
**Topic of Gendre:** Economics/Political Economy/History/Philosophy/Social Science.  
**Overview:**  
"The Wealth of Nations" is a hugely influential book that basically laid the groundwork for modern economics and capitalism, even though it was written way back in 1776. Adam Smith dives into what makes countries prosperous, arguing that it's largely through free markets, the division of labor (where people specialize in tasks), and something he famously called the "invisible hand." This "invisible hand" suggests that when individuals pursue their own self-interest in a competitive market, they unintentionally contribute to the overall good of society. The book champions minimal government interference in the economy, explaining how competition and open trade lead to increased productivity, lower prices, and ultimately, greater wealth for a nation.

## Same as Ever A Guide to What Never Changes
![img](https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/417ycDMhl5L._SY445_SX342_.jpg)
**Topic of Gendre:** Behavioral Economics/Philosophy/History/Decision Making/Life Lessons.  
**Overview:**  
In "Same as Ever," Morgan Housel, much like in "The Psychology of Money," delves into the enduring principles and timeless human behaviors that remain constant across eras, despite the world constantly evolving. He argues that while technology, markets, and trends shift rapidly, fundamental aspects of human nature—like greed, fear, the desire for status, and our biases—remain surprisingly consistent. Through engaging stories and historical anecdotes, Housel highlights these unchanging truths, particularly in relation to finance, investing, and decision-making, offering a powerful perspective that helps readers navigate an uncertain future by understanding the reliable patterns of the past and present.

## Rich Dad Poor Dad: What the Rich Teach Their Kids About Money That the Poor and Middle Class Do Not!
![img](https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/81bsw6fnUiL.jpg)
**Topic of Gendre:** Financial Literacy/Personal Finance/Investing/Mindset/Self-Help.  
**Overview:**  
challenges conventional wisdom about money and wealth, contrasting the financial lessons Robert Kiyosaki learned from his highly educated "poor dad" (his biological father) with those from his uneducated "rich dad" (his best friend's father, who became a millionaire). The book champions financial literacy, emphasizing the critical difference between assets (things that put money in your pocket) and liabilities (things that take money out), and the importance of investing in income-generating assets. Kiyosaki encourages readers to shift their mindset from working for money to having money work for them, advocating for entrepreneurship, real estate, and financial education as paths to true financial freedom rather than relying solely on traditional employment.

## The 48 Laws of Power
![img](https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/611X8GI7hpL._UF1000,1000_QL80_.jpg)
**Topic of Gendre:** Strategy/Psychology/History/Leadership/Manipulation.  
**Overview:**  
is a controversial yet widely read book that distills centuries of history, philosophy, and strategy into blunt, often Machiavellian, advice on how to gain, maintain, and defend power. Robert Greene illustrates each "law" with compelling historical anecdotes and figures, from ancient emperors to cunning courtiers, showcasing the ruthless and often subtle tactics used by those who excel at the game of power. It's not a book about ethics, but rather a guide for understanding the dynamics of control, influence, and manipulation in various social and professional settings, making it a fascinating, if sometimes unsettling, read for anyone interested in human nature and ambition.

## The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People: Powerful Lessons in Personal Change
![img](https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/51G4LdDNUGL._SY445_SX342_.jpg)
**Topic of Gendre:** Self-Help/Personal Development/Productivity/Leadership/Psychology.  
**Overview:**  
is a seminal book that outlines a principle-centered approach to personal and professional effectiveness, arguing that true success comes from aligning your character with universal, timeless principles. Stephen Covey introduces seven interconnected habits, moving from private victories (like being proactive and beginning with the end in mind) to public victories (like thinking win-win and synergizing), all underpinned by continuous improvement. It's not just about quick fixes, but about fundamentally changing your mindset and behaviors to become more effective, productive, and fulfilled in every area of your life.

## How to Analyze People with Dark Psychology: The Ultimate Guide to Read, and Influence Others using Body Language, Manipulation, Subliminal Mind Control, NLP, and Persuasion.

**Overview:**  
This book dives deep into the more covert and less ethical aspects of human psychology, aiming to equip readers with the tools to understand, analyze, and potentially influence others. Jacob Anderson explores various "dark psychology" techniques, including interpreting subtle body language cues, recognizing and applying manipulative tactics, understanding subliminal mind control, utilizing Neuro-Linguistic Programming (NLP), and mastering advanced persuasion strategies. While providing insights into how these methods work, the book is often presented as a guide for self-protection against such influence, allowing readers to recognize when they are being targeted and to use this knowledge for their own strategic advantage in social and professional interactions.

## Master Your Thinking: A Practical Guide to Align Yourself with Reality and Achieve Tangible Results in the Real World
![img](https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/415ksplTc8L._SY445_SX342_.jpg)
**Topic of Gendre:** Self-Help/Mindset/Personal Development/Productivity/Psychology.  
**Overview:**  
"Master Your Thinking" by Thibaut Meurisse is a practical guide focused on the idea that how you think directly shapes your reality and the results you achieve. Meurisse provides actionable strategies to identify and overcome limiting beliefs, cognitive biases, and negative thought patterns that prevent progress. The book emphasizes cultivating a mindset that is grounded in objective reality, enabling readers to make clearer decisions, set more effective goals, and ultimately translate their thoughts into concrete, measurable success in the real world by fostering mental clarity and a results-oriented approach.
